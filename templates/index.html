Sentiment Analysis of the Airline Twitter

What's the analysis about?

Twitter can sometimes have no mercy. But because of that, it is a wonderfully fast litmus test for tracking the customer experience.

We decided to look into Twitter superpowers for analyzing the user experience & brand image within the US airlines industry. On the subset of tweets from February 2015, which are mentioning any of the major 6 US airlines (American, Delta, Southwest, United, US Airways, Virgin) we're builduing a machine learning model which could then be used for fast sentiment analysis at any time. And we are also explaining the relationships in between the collected data.

ML model for estimating the sentiment:

For being able to determine the sentiment of any given tweet in the future which would be mentioning any of the pre-set airlines, we have developed and trained the machine learning model in Scikit-Learn, leveraging its NLP tools.

Our model is classifying the sentiment given the tweet input.

1) First we are defining the stopwords: Combining the classic English stopwords from the nltk.corpus together with the names of all our airlines.

 
# setting up our version of stopwords
airlines = data['airline']
​
# plus classic English stopwords
stop = stopwords.words('english')

2) We are sieving out both of the predefined stopwords groups, and also all the digits, hashtag signs and twitter handles.
 
#extracting only words, other than digits and airline names
def preprocessor(text):
    words = text.split()
    del words[0]
    words = [re.sub('[^0-9a-zA-Z]+', '', w.replace('#','').lower()) for w in words if not w.startswith('http') and not w.startswith('@')]
    finalwords = [w for w in words if (not w.isdigit()  and w not in stop and w not in airlines)]
    return ' '.join(finalwords)

3) We are training the model using the naive bayes algorithm with 2 to 3 ngram range. And when evaluating the predictions on the holdout group, we're achieving 66.5% accuracy.
